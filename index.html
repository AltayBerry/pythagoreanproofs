Proof by Rearrangement

This proof demonstrates the Pythagorean theorem by rearranging four identical right triangles inside a square. The resulting areas of the two smaller squares sum up to the area of the large square.

Euclid's Proof

In Euclid's proof, the theorem is shown by constructing an altitude in the right triangle, which forms two smaller similar triangles. By comparing the areas of the squares on each side, the theorem is established.
