Animated Pythagorean Proofs

Euclid's Proof

This classic proof uses similar triangles to show that in any right triangle with sides a, b and hypotenuse c, the areas of the squares on the legs add up to the area of the square on the hypotenuse.

a² + b² = c²

Proof by Rearrangement

This visual proof involves rearranging copies of the right triangle within a square to illustrate that the sum of the areas of the two smaller squares is equal to the area of the larger square.

a² + b² = c²

Algebraic Proof

This method involves calculating areas algebraically. By constructing two squares (one large and one composed of triangles and a smaller square) and comparing their areas, the Pythagorean relationship is derived.

a² + b² = c²
